## Core Concept
This chapter explains how **changing electric and magnetic fields produce waves** that can travel through space.

* Do not require a medium
* Travel with speed of light
* Carry energy and momentum

---

## Maxwell’s Theory
Electromagnetic waves were predicted by **:contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}**.

* Oscillating electric and magnetic fields
* Fields are perpendicular to each other
* Direction of propagation is perpendicular to both

---

## Nature of EM Waves

* Transverse waves  
* Electric field (E) ⟂ Magnetic field (B)  
* Travel in vacuum  

---

## Speed of EM Waves

:contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

* \( c = 3 \times 10^8 \, m/s \) in vacuum  

---

## Electromagnetic Spectrum

Different types of EM waves arranged by wavelength/frequency:

* Radio Waves  
* Microwaves  
* Infrared  
* Visible Light  
* Ultraviolet  
* X-rays  
* Gamma Rays  

---

## Properties of EM Waves

* Travel in vacuum  
* Exhibit reflection, refraction, diffraction  
* Carry energy  
* Do not require material medium  

---

## Uses of Electromagnetic Waves

### Radio Waves
* Communication (TV, radio)

### Microwaves
* Cooking, radar

### Infrared
* Heat detection, remote controls

### Visible Light
* Vision

### Ultraviolet
* Sterilization

### X-rays
* Medical imaging

### Gamma Rays
* Cancer treatment

---

## Energy of EM Waves

:contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

* Higher frequency → Higher energy
