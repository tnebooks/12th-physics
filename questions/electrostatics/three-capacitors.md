Three capacitors are connected in triangle as shown in the figure. The equivalent capacitance between the points A and C is

```markdown
Between A and C, one capacitor is directly connected and the other two capacitors form a series combination.

The series combination gives

$$
C_s=\frac{C \times C}{C+C}=\frac{C}{2}
$$

This is in parallel with the third capacitor.

Therefore,

$$
C_{eq}=C+\frac{C}{2}
=\frac{3C}{2}
$$

Substituting $C=\frac{4}{3}\,\mu F$ (from the figure),

$$
C_{eq}=2\,\mu F
$$

Hence, the equivalent capacitance between A and C is **2 μF**.
```
