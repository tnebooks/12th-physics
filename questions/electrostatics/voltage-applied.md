  - "Q is doubled, C doubled"
  - "Both Q and C remain same"
answers:
  - "C remains same, Q doubled"
---

If voltage applied on a capacitor is increased from V to 2V, choose the correct conclusion.

```markdown
The capacitance of a capacitor depends only on its geometry and dielectric material. Therefore, increasing the voltage does not change the capacitance.

Using

$$
Q = CV
$$

when the voltage is increased from $V$ to $2V$, the charge stored also doubles.

Hence, **C remains same, Q doubled**.
```
